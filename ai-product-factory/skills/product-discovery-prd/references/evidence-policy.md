# Evidence and Product Decision Policy

## Evidence Levels

### L0 — Unsupported

The idea is based on intuition, a request, or an unverified claim. L0 can justify exploration, not product commitment.

### L1 — Directional

There is credible qualitative or secondary evidence, such as repeated interviews, support patterns, documented incidents, or reliable market research. Bias and representativeness remain material.

### L2 — Behavioral

Observed behavior, product analytics, experiments, pilots, transaction data, or operational records support the problem and expected outcome. Measurement limitations are known.

### L3 — Causal or Repeated

Controlled evidence, repeated production outcomes, strong longitudinal data, or multiple independent behavioral sources support the decision and its expected effect.

Evidence level reflects the strongest relevant support for the critical decision, not the quantity of citations.

## Depth Expectations

| Depth | Typical minimum before GO | Expected work |
|---|---|---|
| lite | L1 when impact is reversible | Existing evidence, affected workflow, essential loop, testable scope |
| standard | L1 with a validation plan; L2 for material commitments | User and competitor evidence, alternatives, system impact, measurable metrics |
| deep | L2; L3 for irreversible or safety-critical claims | Multiple primary sources, scenario analysis, experiments, operational and policy review |

The minimum may increase with cost, irreversibility, safety, regulation, privacy, or strategic impact.

## Decisions

### GO

Proceed when:

- the problem and user are specific;
- the proposed scope closes a valuable loop;
- evidence meets the risk-adjusted threshold;
- success and failure are measurable;
- no critical assumption remains unresolved;
- major system and operational effects are understood.

### PIVOT

Change the user, problem framing, solution shape, channel, scope, or business mechanism when evidence supports the need but contradicts the current approach.

State what changes and which evidence caused the change.

### KILL

Stop when the problem is not material, the target user cannot be reached, expected value is insufficient, constraints make success implausible, or further learning costs more than the decision is worth.

Preserve the evidence and rejected rationale.

### HOLD

Pause when critical evidence, authority, dependency, timing, or capacity is missing.

State the exact evidence or decision that would unblock the work. HOLD is not a soft GO.

## Critical Assumptions

An assumption is critical when its failure would invalidate the user, problem, value, feasibility boundary, adoption path, compliance, or success metric.

For each critical assumption record:

- statement;
- consequence if false;
- current evidence;
- cheapest credible validation;
- owner;
- decision deadline.

## Source Quality

Prefer first-party analytics, direct research, official documentation, primary market data, and reproducible observations. Record dates for changeable claims. Explain inference when combining sources.

Do not present competitor marketing, search ranking, or anecdotal enthusiasm as behavioral proof.
